01 About

Computer science and cognitive science

At Penn I write NLP scrapers and data pipelines, design artificial-language-learning experiments in the Cultural Evolution of Language Lab, and present research to large audiences. My work centers on communication: how people make meaning, and what happens when one side is a machine.

Outside the lab, I'm usually behind a camera.

02 Research

How languages evolve

In the Cultural Evolution of Language Lab, directed by Dr. Gareth Roberts, I study how language structure emerges from interaction. We teach people artificial languages from scratch and watch grammar take shape in real time.

Featured study

Competing Cues in the Emergence of Animacy-Based Grammatical Gender Systems

An artificial-language-learning study of how learners acquire and generalize gender systems organized around animacy. It tests what happens when semantic and distributional cues point in different directions, and which one wins.
